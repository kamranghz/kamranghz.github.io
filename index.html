I am a PhD candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Ottawa, working under the supervision of Professor Abdulmotaleb El Saddik at the MCRLab. My dissertation, CARE-AI, focuses on building coordinated, agentic, robust, and emotion-aware AI systems.

My research sits at the convergence of agentic AI orchestration, Digital Twin technology, and multimodal perception — designing systems that sense, reason, and act across both virtual and physical environments. I am particularly interested in how AI agents can use real-time multimodal signals (vision, speech, biosignals) to provide adaptive, personalized human–AI interaction.

Prior to my PhD, I spent five years as a Software Developer and Blockchain Engineer at Sadad Informatics Corporation (SIC) in Tehran, where I built open-banking architectures, smart contracts, and blockchain-based financial infrastructure at enterprise scale.

02 / Research Interests

Core Research Areas

01
⬡

Agentic AI

Designing autonomous multi-agent systems with A2A (Agent-to-Agent) and MCP (Model Context Protocol) protocols. Integrating LLM-based reasoning with real-time tool use for posture analysis, emotion recognition, and embodied adaptive feedback.

02
◈

Digital Twins

Developing high-fidelity Digital Twin environments using NVIDIA Omniverse, OpenUSD, and Audio2Face. Bridging physical sensor data with virtual simulation for real-time state synchronization and AI-driven immersive XR experiences.

03
◉

Multimodal Systems

Building unified perception pipelines fusing computer vision (MediaPipe, OpenFace), speech emotion models, and wearable biosignals. Enabling AI systems to understand human state through simultaneous multi-channel sensing.

04
⬟

Physical & Embodied AI

Creating LiDAR-based 3D reconstruction pipelines (NeRF, Nerfacto) and XR interaction systems. Exploring how AI agents can operate in and reason about physical environments through embodied sensing and avatar-based interfaces.

03 / Research Vision
"The next frontier of AI is not merely intelligence — it is emotion-aware agency in the physical world: systems that perceive the full human state and respond with genuine, adaptive understanding."

My long-term research vision centers on CARE-AI — Coordinated, Agentic, Robust, Emotion-Aware AI — a framework for building AI systems that go beyond task completion to truly understand and respond to the humans they serve.

I believe this requires three converging capabilities: deep multimodal perception that reads emotion, posture, and physiological state simultaneously; agentic reasoning that coordinates multiple specialized AI agents in real time; and Digital Twin simulation as a safe, high-fidelity environment for training and deploying embodied agents.

Ultimately, I aim to deliver frameworks that bring emotionally intelligent, physically-grounded AI into healthcare, fitness, and human–computer interaction at scale.
